# CS-370-Current-Emerging-Trends-in-Computer-Science

For this project, I worked on building and training a reinforcement learning agent using a Jupyter Notebook. The goal was to apply concepts like neural networks and decision-making to solve a problem through learning rather than direct programming. I was given a base structure that included the environment setup and some helper functions, but I had to implement and adjust the learning process myself. This included working with the Deep Q-Network (DQN), tuning parameters like learning rate and exploration, and understanding how the agent improves over time through trial and error.

One of the biggest things I learned from this course is how computer scientists approach problems differently than traditional programming. Instead of writing exact instructions for every situation, we can design systems that learn patterns and make decisions on their own. This is especially important in real-world situations where problems are too complex to solve with simple logic. Reinforcement learning is a good example of this because the agent learns from experience, similar to how humans learn through practice.

Computer scientists play an important role in developing systems that can solve problems efficiently and at scale. This matters because technology is being used in almost every industry today, from healthcare to finance to transportation. The work we do can improve decision-making, automate tasks, and even help discover new solutions that humans might not think of on their own.

When I approach a problem as a computer scientist, I try to break it down into smaller parts and understand the data and patterns involved. In this project, I had to think about states, actions, and rewards, and how they all connect. Instead of focusing only on getting the “right answer,” I focused more on how the agent learns and improves over time. This mindset shift was important for me because it helped me understand that failure during training is part of the process.

Ethically, I believe computer scientists have a responsibility to make sure the systems they build are safe, fair, and reliable. With AI especially, there is a risk of bias, misuse, or unintended consequences. It is important to think about how these systems affect users and organizations. For example, an AI system making decisions should be transparent and not harm users. As someone learning these skills, I understand that I need to be careful about how technology is applied and always consider the impact it has on others.

Overall, this project helped me connect what I learned in this course to real-world applications. It showed me that reinforcement learning is not just about games, but about solving complex problems in a smarter way. It also gave me a better understanding of how AI systems are built and how they continue to improve over time.
